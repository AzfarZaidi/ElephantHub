Elephants are the largest existing land animals. Three living species are currently recognised:

  • the African bush elephant,
  • the African forest elephant, and
  • the Asian elephant.

Asian elephants differ in several ways from their African relatives, with more than 10 distinct physical differences between them. Elephants need extensive land areas to survive and meet their ecological needs, which includes food, water, and space. On average, an elephant can feed up to 18 hours and consume hundreds of pounds of plant matter in a single day. As a result, as they lose habitat, they often come into conflict with people in competition for resources
